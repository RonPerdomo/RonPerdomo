I didn't start in tech. I started in a firehouse.

Five years with the FDNY. When the call came in, it didn't matter how tired you were — muscle memory kicked in, and you operated as a team with one goal. That environment taught me something I carry into everything I do: prepare properly, stay focused, push through.

Before that, I spent three years at the NYC Law Department. Everything was confidential, everything ran on deadlines, and being good wasn't enough — you had to be accurate, accountable, and fast. They pulled me into QA, trusted me with the most sensitive case files, and had me train new hires. I learned that in a high-stakes environment, one mistake matters.

I'm making this move into cybersecurity because I want a career with no ceiling. GRC, IAM, and SOC work reward the same things I've spent years building: discipline, documentation, and the ability to stay sharp when the pressure is on.

Currently at Spectrum managing 1,000+ support tickets weekly while studying NIST CSF 2.0, ISO 27001, and SOC 2 — and building this portfolio under the mentorship of a CIO with 20+ years in GRC, IAM, and Enterprise Controls.
